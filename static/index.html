Monte Carlo Ray Sampling

Traditional acoustic modeling often uses limited ray paths. Our system employs Monte Carlo sampling to generate thousands of unique trajectories, ensuring that every corner of an irregular geometry is explored.

Markovian State Transitions

CORE NOVELTY

Unlike deterministic models where a ray simply reflects, our Markov engine treats each interaction as a probabilistic state. A ray might reflect, scatter, or be absorbed based on a transition matrix defined by the surface material.

Irregular Geometries

Simple box rooms are rare in reality. By using Trimesh-based collision detection, we simulate sound propagation in complex, non-convex spaces (like L-shaped halls or curved auditoriums) where sound shadowing and secondary reflections are critical.

Geometric Realism

Research Summary

The combination of stochastic sampling and probabilistic transitions provides a more realistic approximation of diffuse sound fields than standard ray-tracing. This approach is particularly effective for predicting reverberation in spaces with highly varied materials.
